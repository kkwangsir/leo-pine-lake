Leo's family was camping at Pine Lake for the first time. While Dad set up the tent and Mom unpacked the cooler, Leo spotted a rabbit — small, brown, with a twitchy nose.

"Come here, little guy!" he whispered, and crawled after it into the trees. The rabbit hopped. Leo followed. Deeper and deeper into the green shadows.

1

The rabbit disappeared. Leo looked around — the trees all looked the same. "Hello? Hello!" No answer. His heart started beating fast. The sun was going down, and the forest was getting darker.

He sat down under a big oak tree and hugged his knees. He was lost.

2

Then he remembered what Dad always said: "If you're lost, STOP. Sit down, Think, Observe, and Plan." Leo took a deep breath. He had a whistle on his backpack strap. He blew three short blasts.

No answer. "Okay," he said to himself. "Plan B."

3

He found a small stream. Dad had told him that water flows downhill, and downhill leads to people. He followed the stream, stepping carefully over wet rocks.

A deer watched him from the other side. "Hi, deer," Leo said. The deer dipped its head and walked away.

4

He came to a clearing — flat ground, soft moss, a fallen tree. He decided to make a shelter.

He leaned branches against the fallen tree to make a roof, then covered them with ferns and leaves. Inside, he spread dry moss to sleep on. It wasn't a hotel, but it was safe.

5

Leo was hungry. He remembered that dandelions are edible — the whole plant. He found some near the stream, washed them, and ate the leaves.

They were bitter, but his stomach stopped growling. "Wild salad," he said, and almost laughed.

6

The night was cold. Leo found clear ground, circled it with stones, and gathered dry twigs and bark. He took the lens from his sunglasses and focused sunlight onto dry bark.

Nothing happened. Then — a tiny curl of smoke. He blew gently. A flame.

7

The fire crackled and popped, sending orange sparks into the dark sky. Leo added bigger sticks. The warmth felt like a hug.

He sat by the fire, watching the stars between the treetops, and sang his favorite campfire song quietly. He wasn't scared anymore — he was a survivor.

8

Morning. Leo woke to birds singing. He put more wood on the fire to keep smoke rising. Then he used his shoes to make big letters in the clearing:

H E L P — visible from the sky. He sat down next to the letters and waited.

9

A low hum grew louder. A helicopter appeared over the treetops! Leo waved his red jacket and blew his whistle as loud as he could.

The helicopter hovered, then slowly came down. A rescue worker jumped out, smiling. "Good job, kid. You did everything right."

10

Back at camp, Mom burst into tears and hugged him so tight he couldn't breathe. Dad held him for a long time. "I'm proud of you," Dad said. "You remembered everything we practiced."

Leo felt warm inside. He was a boy who survived.

11

At home, Leo pinned a paper on his bedroom wall:

LEO'S WILDERNESS RULES
1. STOP — sit and think
2. Find water and follow it
3. Make shelter before dark
4. Fire = signal
5. Be brave, you're stronger than you know

He smiled. He couldn't wait for the next camping trip.

Answer Key

1. B — He followed a rabbit into the woods

Leo saw a rabbit and crawled after it into the trees (page 1). He was just curious, not trying to run away.

2. C — He blew his whistle and called for help

Leo first blew three short blasts on his whistle and called out (page 3). Only when nobody answered did he move to Plan B.

3. C — His dad told him water flows downhill to people

Leo remembered his dad's advice: water flows downhill, and downhill leads to people (page 4).

4. B — A place that protects you from weather

Leo built a shelter using branches, ferns, and leaves to protect himself from the cold night air (page 5).

5. B — Safe to eat

Edible means safe to eat. Leo knew dandelions are edible — the whole plant can be eaten (page 6).

6. C — He used the lens from his sunglasses to focus sunlight

Leo took the lens out of his sunglasses and focused sunlight onto dry bark until it started smoking (page 7).

7. B — A type of green plant with feathery leaves

Ferns are green plants with feathery, divided leaves that grow in forests (page 5).

8. B — He made a HELP sign with his shoes and kept the fire smoking

Leo kept the fire smoking and used his shoes to make the word HELP in the clearing, visible from the sky (page 9).

9. B — It stayed in one place in the air

To hover means to stay suspended in one place in the air. The helicopter hovered before coming down (page 10).

10. B — Stay with family, stay calm, use survival skills

Leo's rules at the end show the main lesson: stay calm, use survival skills, and be brave (page 12). He was excited for the next trip, not scared.
